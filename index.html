[;9] Blog course III

SEO concepts and methodologies:
  • Discover keywords using "Keywords Everywhere" and "KeySearch".
  • Niche down to a keyword you can rank for
  • Find tail keywords
  • Write the post and include those keywords in strategic places like:
    • Meta title and description
    • Subheadlines
    • First sentence
    • Anchor texts on internal links from other related posts
    • Image file names and alt tags
    • Paragraphs
  • Getting people to link to your content
  • External links
  • Craft a title people will want to click on. It should be descriptive, succinct, and accurate
  • Find keywords with 300-1,000 search volume and a "KeySearch Score" of 35 or less than that.

[;10] Blog course IV

Tips to make a piece of web-friendly content:
  • Headline is king, so spark interest, be concise and don't lie. Try many versions.
  • Make your post easier to scan with varied formatting. Call-out boxes for important points and take-aways.
  • Shorten the whole post. Establish the answer to "Why should I care?".
  • Internal cliff-hangers. Short and standalone sentence that surprises people or draws them to read the next sentence or paragraph for an explanation. Use it sparingly, you can create suspense as you tell your story
  • Make it relatable. Not to be "perfect" in the eyes of your readers, be real.
  • Use humor.Great content entertains, educates, and inspires.
  • Be specific.Avoid writing about talking about "some people". Share your story.
  • End with a call-to-action.Leave them wondering, "What's next?". Ask them to share your post. Email to you to chatting.

[;12] Blog course VI

Polish your post and get feedback!
List of what you need to do before you publish your blog post:
  • Your canonical tag tells Google who the original source of a piece of content is.
  • Google Analytics it's to understand which content is popular and what distribution channels are working.
  • Google Search Console
  • Your social sharing metadata.
  • Your email list is set up and you have a form embedded on your blog post.
  • Spellcheck and grammar check.
  • Revisit your title again.
  • Ask a friend for feedback.
  • Set one day for general review and other only for polish.
