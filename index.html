7. Blog course I

How to make a web-friendly content:
  • Buy a domain.
  • Headline is king, so spark interest, be concise and don't lie. Try many versions.
  • Make your post easier to scan with varied formatting.
  • Shorten the whole post. Ask yourself "Why should I care?".
  • Internal cliff-hangers. Short and standalone sentence that surprises people or draws them to read the next sentence for an explanation. is used to create suspense as you tell your story
  • Make it relatable. Not to be "perfect" in the eyes of your readers, be real.
  • Use humor.Great content entertains, educates, and inspires.
  • Be specific.Share your story not others.
  • End with a call-to-action. Leave them wondering, "What's next?". Ask them to share your post. Email to you.
  • Use challenges to inspire people to action.
  • Giving people an incentive to sign up.
  • The link could be shared out of context.
  • Create content that demonstrates an intimate understanding of your audience.
  • Email lists are powerful, and you should start one. I recommend "ConvertKit".
  • Makes your reader feel like you're speaking directly to them.

8. Blog course II

SEO concepts and methodologies:
  • Add self resirecting canonical URL, it tells Google who the original source of a piece of content is.
  • Maintain an idea log.
  • Determine if the best medium is Social media (entertain) or Search (look for info or solve a problem).
  • Validate idea with research. Avoid social media trends and popular people content.
  • Discover keywords using "Keywords Everywhere" and "KeySearch".
  • Niche down to a keyword you can rank for
  • Find tail keywords
  • Write the post and include those keywords in strategic places like:
    • Meta title and description
    • Sections and titles
    • First sentence
    • Anchor texts on internal links from other related posts
  • Getting people to link to your content
  • External links
  • Craft a title people will want to click on. It should be descriptive, succinct, and accurate
  • Find keywords with 300-1,000 search volume and a "KeySearch Score" of 35 or less than that.

9. Blog course III

List of what you need to do before you publish your blog post:
  • Your canonical tag.
  • Check Google Analytics (to know which content is popular and what distribution channels are working.)
  • Check Google Search Console (GSC)
  • Have a way to sign up.
  • Spellcheck and grammar check.
  • Review your title again.
  • Ask for feedback.
  • Set a day for a review and to check details.
  • Submit your sitemap in GSC.
  • Comment your article on LinkedIn and Reddit.
  • Send article to email list.

10. How to write

This are the neccessary things to do in order to hook, write and show your work:
  • Your voice is what you know plus your opinion.
  • Active voice
  • Story undertable by foreigners
  • Interact with the reader
  • Ask and answers that the reader would have
  • Use keywords in titles mainly
  • Trick your audience to hook but not to retain
  • Start, problem, solution storytelling
  • Be Simple
  • Bigger picture to details
  • Contrast now with a better future
  • Summarize at the end of any section
  • small (keypoints), big (story), medium (summary)
  • To talk truth anonymously use concepts insteads of facts
